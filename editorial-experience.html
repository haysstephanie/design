Elon News Network

The Battery Man | Print design, web design and social media design

The Battery Man was a six-week-long project about an Elon freshman who designed and built his own electric motorcycles. I traveled to the student's home twice with Elon News Network reporter Anton L. Delgado to take photos and help record interviews. I also coded the website, made the graphics and designed the printed product.

This was a very collaborative project. Anton and I discussed a wide variety of graphics we wanted and drew out sketched prototypes of the webpage before I built it. We went through multiple iterations and thoroughly edited each other's work.

Elon News Network

Best Of 2017 | Print design, web design, social media design and planning

I led and managed the Elon News Network's first Best of special edition. This semester-long project entailed working with ENN Web Producer Alex Hager and ENN Managing Editor Emmanuel Morgan to determine categories, create a survey, encourage student voting, tally results and assign articles and photos to reporters.

The most gratifying part of this experience was getting to start a brand new tradition within Elon News Network. They continued with the section the year after and have plans to do it this year as well.

Sacramento Business Journal

CFO of the Year | Print design, web design and social media design

The CFO of the Year theme was "Build your own CFO," to wide variety of attributes that the role now encompasses. I decided on the theme a month in advance and pitched it to my editor, who was enthusiastic about the idea. We asked each CFO to tell us the top three qualities a CFO needs, and I came up with a visual for one of those qualities for each honoree. Each visual was photographed with a plain manikin for an inside spread, and, for the cover, was styled as a kit of a generic CFO with accessories.

Women Who Mean Business | Print design, web design and social media design

The theme for Women Who Mean Business was "A lasting impression." The theme was decided on a couple months in advance, giving me the time to design and order custom stamps for each honoree. To create the cover, each woman stamped their name on a sheet of paper that had the "WWMB" logo on it, and a photo was taken of that piece of paper. The wraparound is a composite image, and the shot of the stamps and ink pads was merged with the photo of paper. I worked with our photographer to decide on black and white photos to give the portraits a timeless look and let the color of the stamps pop.

I also carried the theme across to two GIFs for Instagram and Twitter, and designed the webpages for each of the honorees, which included hand-coded pull quotes.
